About

Built for the complexity
of modern law.

Nandini Shrivastava is a first-year law student at JECRC University, Jaipur, whose record already reflects what most students spend years building. A published researcher, a moot court speaker, and a contributor across five legal organisations, she operates at the intersection of scholarship and practice.

Her focus spans corporate law, where she examines governance structures and director liability; international law, where she traces the gap between treaty commitments and enforcement reality; and environmental law, where she argues for constitutional environmental rights as a matter of urgency, not aspiration.

At Jus Corpus, Clear Law, Lawyers Society, and Kachhawa & Associates, she has written, researched, and argued. The work speaks for itself.
